Frequently Asked Questions

🔍

A valid passport is required for all visitors to Taniti. Citizens of most countries can stay for up to 90 days without a visa. Check with your local embassy for specific requirements.

The official currency is the Taniti Dollar (T$). US Dollars are widely accepted, and ATMs are available throughout the island. Credit cards are accepted at most hotels and restaurants.

English is widely spoken in tourist areas, hotels, and restaurants. Many locals speak English fluently, especially in Taniti City and Merriton Landing.

Tap water is generally safe to drink in most areas, but bottled water is recommended for visitors. All hotels provide filtered or bottled water.

The dry season (June-October) offers the best weather for outdoor activities. The island is beautiful year-round, but expect more rain during the wet season (November-May).

Taniti uses standard 120V outlets compatible with US plugs. Most hotels provide both US and European style adapters.

Taniti has a modern hospital in Taniti City and several clinics. Travel insurance is recommended. Pharmacies are well-stocked with both local and imported medications.

WiFi is available at all hotels and many restaurants. Cellular data works well throughout the island. Internet speeds are good in populated areas.

Tipping is appreciated but not mandatory. 10-15% is customary at restaurants. Taxi drivers and hotel staff appreciate small tips for good service.

Pets are allowed but require special permits and quarantine procedures. Contact the Taniti Agriculture Department for requirements. Most hotels have pet-friendly options.
