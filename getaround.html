Getting Around Taniti

From public buses to rental cars, explore the island your way with reliable transportation options.

🚌

Public Bus

Extensive bus network covering the entire island. Buses run regularly from 5am to 11pm daily.

Cost: $1 per ride
Frequency: Every 15-30 minutes
Coverage: Island-wide
🚕

Taxis

Available 24/7 throughout the island. Can be hailed on the street or called ahead.

Cost: $2 base + $1/km
Availability: 24 hours
Booking: Phone or street hail
🚲

Bike Rental

Perfect for exploring at your own pace. Rentals available at hotels and major locations.

Cost: $5/day
Locations: Hotels & bike shops
Best for: Short distances
🏍️

Scooter Rental

Popular choice for locals and visitors. Easy to maneuver through island roads.

Cost: $10/day
License: Valid driver's license
Best for: Flexible travel
🚗

Car Rental

Full-size vehicles available for rent. Perfect for families or those carrying gear.

Cost: $40-60/day
Locations: Airport & hotels
Requirements: Valid license
🚐

Shuttle Service

Scheduled shuttles between major attractions. Great for organized tours.

Cost: $5-15 per person
Routes: Tourist attractions
Booking: Through hotels

Transportation Tips

🗺️

Island Layout

Taniti is small enough to explore thoroughly. Most attractions are within 30-45 minutes of each other.

⏰

Best Times

Bus service is most frequent during peak tourist season. Early morning and evening buses may be less crowded.

💰

Cost Saving

Public buses are the most economical option. Consider a multi-day bus pass for frequent travelers.

🌧️

Weather Considerations

Roads are well-maintained but can be slippery during rain. Scooters and bikes are not recommended in heavy rain.

📱

Apps & Booking

Download the Taniti Transport app for real-time bus schedules and taxi booking.

🚶‍♀️

Walking

Taniti City and Merriton Landing are very walkable. Many attractions are within walking distance of accommodations.
