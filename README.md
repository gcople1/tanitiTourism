# Island of Taniti Tourism Website

## Project Overview
This project is a comprehensive tourism website designed for the island of Taniti. It serves as a one-stop digital hub for visitors, providing essential information on travel logistics, accommodations, local dining, activities, and island-specific FAQs. The site is structured to be user-friendly with a clear navigation system and interactive widgets to assist in trip planning.

## Site Architecture
The website is organized into several key sections accessible via a primary navigation menu :
***Home/Landing Page:** A welcoming entry point featuring a high-level overview and quick access to major site categories.
***Travel:** Information on arriving at Taniti by plane or ship, including booking widgets and details on airport expansions .
***Stay (Accommodations):** Detailed sections for hotels, resorts, and hostels with integrated booking widgets and location maps .
***Eat (Food):** A guide to local dining categorized by location (Taniti City, Merriton Landing) and cuisine type (Local, Pan-Asian, American), as well as grocery information.
***Play (Activities):** A robust catalog of things to do, filterable by activity type (Outdoor, Arts & Culture, Guided Tours) or location.
***Get Around (Transportation):** Logistics for island travel, including public buses, taxis, rideshares, car rentals, and bicycle rentals (with legal helmet requirements).
***Ask! (FAQ & Contact):** Essential visitor information and a communication portal for support.

## Key Features
***Interactive Booking Widgets:** Users can book flights, cruises, and various types of lodging directly through the site.
***Dynamic Mapping:** Multiple pages feature draggable and zoomable maps with marked locations to help visitors navigate attractions and services.
***Categorized Activity Discovery:** Includes a "Rainy Day Fun" section to ensure visitors have options regardless of weather.
**Essential Visitor Logistics:** The FAQ section provides critical data on:
    ***Currency:** US Dollar is the standard, though Euros and Yen are often accepted.
    ***Power:** 120-volt outlets (US standard).
    ***Laws & Safety:** Information on drinking age (18), alcohol service hours, and safety tips regarding petty crime.
    ***Language:** Guidance on where English is fluently spoken versus rural areas where it is less common.

## User Experience (UX) Design
**Navigation:** The site utilizes a consistent navigation bar and a "hamburger menu" for mobile accessibility.
**Site Flow:** The architecture ensures that users can move from high-level information (Landing Page) to specific external booking tasks with minimal clicks .
***Breadcrumbs:** Pages are designed with clear headers to help users identify their current location within the site hierarchy.
